Planting Guide

Soil Requirements

  • Clay loam or silt loam soils with good water retention
  • pH range: 5.5-6.5 (slightly acidic)
  • Soil should have good organic matter (2-3%)
  • Fields should be level for uniform water distribution

Climate Conditions

  • Temperature: 20-35°C (ideal 25-30°C)
  • Annual rainfall: 150-300 cm
  • Requires full sunlight (6-8 hours daily)
  • High humidity (70-80%) during growth period

Planting Process

1

Land Preparation

Plow field 2-3 times to fine tilth, level properly, and construct bunds (15-20cm height) around field.

2

Nursery Preparation

Soak seeds for 12 hours, incubate for 24 hours. Sow 1kg seeds/25m² nursery area. Maintain 2-3cm water depth.

3

Transplanting

Transplant 21-25 day old seedlings at 2-3 seedlings/hill, 20x15cm spacing when nursery is 25-30cm tall.

Harvesting & Post-Harvest

Harvest Timing

Harvest when 80-85% of grains turn straw color (30-35 days after flowering). Moisture content should be 20-25%.

Harvest Methods

  • Manual harvesting with sickle (common for basmati)
  • Combine harvesters can be used for large fields
  • Cut 15-20cm above ground level to maintain quality
    Drying

    Spread in thin layers (5-7cm) on clean surface for 2-3 sunny days

    Threshing

    Manual beating or mechanical threshers at 400-500 rpm

    Storage

    Store at 12-14% moisture in clean, rat-proof godowns

Pest & Disease Management

Major Pests

Stem Borer

White heads appear during milk stage. Larvae bore into stems.

Control: Remove infected plants, spray Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC (15ml/10L water) at 30 and 50 DAT

Brown Plant Hopper

Causes "hopper burn" - yellowing and drying from field margins.

Control: Avoid excess nitrogen, spray Imidacloprid 17.8% SL (0.3ml/L water), maintain 5cm water for 3-4 days

Common Diseases

Blast (Pyricularia oryzae)

Diamond-shaped lesions on leaves, neck rot causes blank panicles.

Control: Grow resistant varieties, avoid excess N, spray Tricyclazole 75% WP (0.6g/L

Bacterial Leaf Blight

Yellow water-soaked lesions spreading from leaf tips.

Control: Avoid wounding plants, spray Streptocycline 500ppm + Copper oxychloride 0.3%
